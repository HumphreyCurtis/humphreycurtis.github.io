Alongside my academic research I design and build software — primarily mobile and wearable applications for people with disabilities, and AI-powered tools for navigating public services. The projects below span research prototypes and deployed products.

Portfolio

  • Advokit
    Web · Generative AI · Co-design · 2024–present

    An inclusive, GenAI-enhanced digital toolkit co-designed with people with aphasia to support access to public services. Advokit includes tools for benefit navigation, community storytelling, and plain-language assistance — addressing the systemic barriers that prevent disabled people from accessing entitlements they are owed. Built in collaboration with King's College London and Aphasia Re-Connect, and live at advokit.uk.

  • Aphasia Smartwatch AAC App
    Android · Wear OS · Kotlin · 2022–present

    A phrase-based augmentative and alternative communication application for Wear OS smartwatches, co-designed through participatory research with stroke survivors with aphasia. Enables discreet, wrist-based communication support as a lightweight alternative to tablet-based AAC — always on the user's body, operable with one hand. Currently being open-sourced as part of a wider wearable AAC portfolio.

    GitHub Related research
  • Aphasia Smartbadge AAC App
    Android · E-ink display · Kotlin · 2022–2023

    A wearable AAC application for smart badge devices — worn on the chest and visible to communication partners. Designed to complement the smartwatch app by providing a larger, outward-facing display that can signal communication needs at a glance. Evaluated through co-design sessions with people with aphasia in London communities. Described in the "Breaking Badge" paper at ASSETS 2023.

  • Microsoft Teams Remote Meeting Agent
    Microsoft Teams SDK · Azure AI · Python · 2024

    A research prototype of an AI agent for Microsoft Teams designed to support chronically ill people in maintaining employment and social presence during video calls. Built during a research internship at the Microsoft Research Ability Team in Redmond, working under Martez Mott. The agent handles presence, participation, and communication scaffolding for users who cannot reliably attend synchronous meetings in person.

  • Smartglasses AAC Prototypes
    Mixed Reality · Unity · Android · 2023–2024

    A series of augmented reality prototypes exploring smartglasses as discreet AAC communication aids. Developed through participatory co-design with people with aphasia, testing how AR overlays could provide phrase suggestion, conversation support, and communication logging via a head-worn display. The research ultimately identified significant cognitive load constraints with current hardware — findings published in Scientific Reports.
