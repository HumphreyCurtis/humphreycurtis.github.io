State of the Art in AAC

Systematic review · Taxonomy · 2021–2022 · ASSETS 2022, Athens

When I began my PhD, I needed to understand the field I was entering — and I found that no comprehensive map of high-tech AAC research existed. This project set out to create one. Working through 562 articles drawn from ACM Digital Library and SCOPUS, I conducted a systematic review of the entire published landscape of AAC device research, producing a taxonomy that organises the field by interaction modality, device form factor, community of focus, and methodological approach.

The taxonomy revealed patterns that were striking and, in some cases, troubling: an overwhelming focus on technology development over user evaluation; a narrow conception of AAC users that excluded many populations actually using these devices; and a persistent gap between the laboratory prototypes filling conference proceedings and the real-world needs of people with complex communication needs. These findings directly shaped the research programme I pursued across my PhD.

The paper was presented at ASSETS 2022 in Athens, where I was also a recipient of the SIGACCESS Travel Scholarship. It has since been cited as a foundational reference by researchers entering the AAC space and is the first publication in my portfolio — the starting point for everything that followed.
